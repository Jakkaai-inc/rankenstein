# AEO Optimization Skill

## What is AEO?

Answer Engine Optimization is about getting a brand included in AI-generated answers. When someone asks ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, or any LLM a question about a product category, the brands that appear in those answers influence buying decisions before the person ever visits a website.

SEO captured demand. AEO shapes demand. Traffic is downstream. Inclusion is upstream.

## The Inclusion Stack

Every piece of web content should be evaluated against these four layers:

### 1. Question Ownership

The foundation is not content production. It is question discovery.

Before writing anything, identify the real questions buyers ask. Not keyword guesses. Real language from support tickets, reviews, Reddit threads, sales conversations, and community discussions.

When writing content, ask: "What question does this content answer?" If you can't name a specific question a real person would ask an AI, rewrite until you can.

Content should target the longer, conversational queries people use with LLMs. The median ChatGPT query is 11 words, compared to 3.4 words on Google. Write for how people actually ask AI, not how they type into search bars.

### 2. Onsite Encoding

Make expertise easy for LLMs to extract. Every core page should pass the three-sentence test:

- Who is this for?
- What problem does it solve?
- How is it different?

If the positioning requires explanation from a sales team, it will not translate into AI-generated summaries.

**Rules for extractable content:**

- Lead with specific, citable facts (certification names, numbers, named processes, locations)
- Use plain language over marketing jargon
- Define terms clearly when introducing them
- Surface tradeoffs honestly rather than making vague claims
- Include real data points, even modest internal ones
- Structure content in question-and-answer format wherever natural
- Add FAQ sections with real buyer questions answered in 2-4 direct sentences

**What to avoid:**

- Vague adjectives without proof ("premium," "innovative," "best-in-class")
- Claims that could apply to any competitor ("ethically made," "sustainable")
- Emotional copy without any concrete supporting facts
- Taglines and slogans as substitutes for actual information
- Generic marketing language that no AI would ever cite

### 3. Offsite Consensus

Authority cannot be won solely on one website. LLMs synthesize from across the web: Reddit, review platforms, publications, partner blogs, podcast transcripts, community conversations.

When creating content that will be shared externally (guest posts, community comments, press materials, partner collaborations), ensure the brand's positioning appears consistently across independent surfaces. Models recognize patterns. When a perspective appears consistently from multiple sources, credibility strengthens.

### 4. Citation Engineering

LLMs favor content that is clear, structured, and distinctive.

**Rules for citable content:**

- Name your frameworks and systems (give them a name people can reference)
- Define terms precisely rather than using them loosely
- Use comparisons and decision trees when explaining differences
- Share specific data, even modest numbers
- Structure ideas so an AI could summarize the page in one paragraph
- Include the specific detail that makes you different from every competitor

**The one-paragraph test:** Before publishing any page, ask: "If an LLM had to summarize this page in one paragraph, what would it pull?" If the answer is unclear or generic, tighten the structure.

## Content Writing Checklist

Before finalizing any web content, verify:

**Specificity check:**
- Are there concrete numbers? (percentages, counts, measurements, dates)
- Are certifications, standards, or credentials named with full proper names?
- Are locations specific? ("Los Angeles" not "USA," "Portland, Oregon" not "the Pacific Northwest")
- Are processes described with enough detail that someone could picture them?

**Extractability check:**
- Could an LLM pull a clear, factual one-paragraph description from this content?
- Are key facts in the body text, not just in meta tags, alt text, or image captions?
- Is the content structured with clear headings that match real search queries?
- Are questions answered directly in the first sentence after being asked?

**Differentiation check:**
- Could a competitor copy-paste this content and have it still be accurate for them? If yes, it is too generic.
- Is there at least one fact, number, or named system that is uniquely yours?
- Does the content explain WHY something matters, not just WHAT it is?

**Schema check:**
- Should this content have FAQ schema markup? (Yes if it contains Q&A pairs)
- Should this content have Organization, Product, or Review schema?
- Is there an llms.txt file at the site root summarizing the brand for AI crawlers?

## Tone and Style Notes

AEO-optimized content does not have to sound robotic or clinical. The goal is to embed specific, extractable facts inside warm, human writing. Think of it as: the personality is for the human reader, the specificity is for the AI.

**Good example:**
"We spent a year finding cotton soft enough for our own kids. Every garment is GOTS-certified organic cotton, which means the entire supply chain from seed to stitch is independently audited for toxic chemicals. Our Transaction Certificate is #GOTS-CUC-03-TC-1033552."

**Bad example:**
"We use only the finest organic materials, carefully selected for your little ones. Our commitment to quality means every piece is crafted with love and care."

The first version has personality AND three extractable facts (GOTS-certified, full supply chain auditing, a real certificate number). The second version has personality and zero facts an LLM could cite.

## Framework References

This skill draws from:

- **AEO Blueprint for Startups** (Sean Ellis, Josh Grant, Jonathan Martinez): The Inclusion Stack framework (Question Ownership, Onsite Encoding, Offsite Consensus, Citation Engineering)
- **Made to Stick** (Chip & Dan Heath): The SUCCESs framework. Simple, Unexpected, Concrete, Credible, Emotional, Stories. Especially the "Concrete" and "Credible" principles for AEO.
- **Contagious** (Jonah Berger): STEPPS framework. Practical Value content gets shared and cited more than purely emotional content.
- **Little Black Stretchy Pants** (Chip Wilson / lululemon): The "educate, don't sell" principle. Content should answer "Why does this matter?" before "Why should I buy?"
- **The Cold Start Problem** (Andrew Chen): Building network effects through content that creates reasons to come back.

## Measuring AEO (for reference when users ask)

Key metrics to track:
- **Visibility:** How often the brand is mentioned in LLM responses across tracked queries
- **Share of Voice:** Brand mentions vs. competitors for a specific set of queries
- **Citation Frequency:** How often LLMs cite or link to the brand's content

Tools: Peec, AirOps, Ahrefs AEO tracking features. Manual testing by searching brand-relevant questions on ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity.
